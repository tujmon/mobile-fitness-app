# The Hacker's Diet — Fitness Ladder Strategy

Based on *"The Hacker's Diet"* by John Walker — [Chapter: What, Me Exercise?](https://www.fourmilab.ch/hackdiet/e4/exercise.html)

---

## Core Philosophy

- Exercise is **not** primarily for burning calories — it is for **living longer** and **feeling better**.
- Even 15 minutes of daily exercise can yield a net gain of ~1,000 extra days of life by age 65.
- The program is designed around six principles:

1. **Minimum time per day** — 10 to 15 minutes.
2. **Time does not increase** as you progress.
3. **Easy to start**, regardless of current fitness level.
4. **No pain** — stop if you feel pain.
5. **Progress at your own pace**.
6. **No equipment needed** — exercise anywhere, in private if you like.

---

## The Fitness Ladder Structure

The program is organized as a **48-rung fitness ladder** divided into two phases:

| Phase | Rungs | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Introductory Ladder** | 1–15 | Easier variants of each exercise; builds foundational strength. |
| **Lifetime Ladder** | 16–48 | Full versions of each exercise; long-term fitness maintenance. |

Each rung prescribes a specific number of repetitions for **five exercises**, done **every day**.

---

## The Five Exercises

### 1. Bend

**Introductory variant:**
Stand upright with legs apart, hands outstretched above your head. Bend forward as far as you can, trying to touch your toes. Straighten up and bend backward moderately. Repeat.

**Lifetime variant (Bend and Bounce):**
Stand upright with legs apart, hands outstretched above your head. Bend forward and touch the floor between your legs, bounce up a few inches, and touch the floor again. Straighten up and bend backward. Repeat.

### 2. Sit Up

**Introductory variant:**
Lie on your back, feet slightly apart, hands at your side. Lift your head and shoulders off the floor far enough to see your heels. Smoothly lower back down. Repeat.

**Lifetime variant:**
Lie on your back, feet slightly apart, hands at your side. Lift your upper body, bending at the waist, until sitting up vertically. Keep arms at sides and feet on the floor — no cheating with arms or legs. Smoothly lower back down. Repeat.

### 3. Leg Lift

**Introductory variant:**
Lie face down, legs slightly apart, palms under your thighs. Lift your left leg (bending at hip and knee) while simultaneously lifting your head. Lower both. Then lift your right leg and head the same way. Each repetition counts both legs.

**Lifetime variant (Prone Lift):**
Lie face down, legs slightly apart, palms under your thighs. Lift **both legs** at least high enough that your thighs lift from your hands. Simultaneously lift your head and shoulders. Smoothly lower everything back down. Repeat.

### 4. Push Up

**Introductory variant:**
Lie face down, palms just outside shoulders, arms bent. Keeping your **knees on the floor** and allowing legs to bend at the knee, lift your upper body until arms are straight. Lower back down. Repeat.

**Lifetime variant:**
Lie face down, palms just outside shoulders, arms bent. Keeping your **entire body straight** (no knees on the floor), lift your body until arms are straight. Lower back down. Repeat.

### 5. Run and Jump

**Introductory variant:**
Run in place at a brisk pace, lifting legs 4–6 inches from the floor. Count a step only when your **left foot** touches the floor. Every 75 steps, stop and do **7 introductory jumping jacks**: stand with legs together, arms at side; jump extending legs to the side and arms outward to shoulder level; jump back to start.

**Lifetime variant:**
Run in place at a brisk pace, lifting legs 4–6 inches from the floor. Every 75 steps, stop and do **10 full jumping jacks**: same motion as introductory but with full extension.

**Counting system:** The "Count" column uses two numbers. The first = number of complete sets (75 steps + jumps). The second = extra running steps after the last full set (no jumps after these).

## Progression Rules

1. **Always start at Rung 1**, no matter your fitness level.
2. **Spend at least 5 days on each rung** — ideally **one full week**.
3. On a chosen day each week, **try the next rung**:
   - If it feels as easy as the previous rung felt the week before → **move up**.
   - If you have difficulty completing any exercise, can't finish in 15 minutes, or feel pain/exhaustion → **stay at your current rung** for another week.
4. Repetitions are **reduced** when transitioning from rung 15 (top of introductory) to rung 16 (start of lifetime) to compensate for increased difficulty.

## App Design Implications

### Key Features to Implement

- **Current Rung Tracker** — Store the user's current rung, phase (introductory vs lifetime), and date started.
- **Daily Workout Guide** — Show today's 5 exercises with exact repetition counts for the user's current rung.
- **Exercise Timer & Counter** — Built-in step counter for the run/jump exercise; auto-count sets.
- **Progression System** — Weekly "try next rung" prompt with a pass/fail assessment (pain, time > 15 min, inability to complete).
- **Minimum 5-day lock** — Prevent advancing before spending at least 5 days on a rung.
- **Exercise Instructions** — Illustrated/described variants for introductory vs lifetime versions of each exercise.
- **Streak & History** — Track daily completion, current streak, and rung history over time.
- **No Equipment Reminder** — Reinforce that no gym or equipment is needed.

### Data Model (per user)

```
- currentRung: number (1–48)
- phase: "introductory" | "lifetime"
- rungStartDate: date
- dailyLog: [{ date, rung, completed }]
- assessmentLog: [{ date, rung, passed, notes }]
```

### Workout Duration

All rungs are designed to be completed in **10–15 minutes**, regardless of level. This stays constant from rung 1 to rung 48.
